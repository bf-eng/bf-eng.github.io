Facts about Cats

  • Cats are very independent creatures and do not need as much attention as dogs.
  • Cats are crepuscular, which means that they are most active at dawn and dusk.
  • Cats can see in the dark because they have a special layer of cells in their eyes called the tapetum lucidum.
  • Cats have 32 muscles in each of their ears, which allows them to rotate their ears in any direction.
  • Cats can purr when they are happy, content, or relaxed.
  • Cats can also purr when they are in pain or injured.
  • Cats are very good at climbing and can climb trees, walls, and even fences.
  • Cats are also very good at jumping and can jump up to three times their own height.
  • Cats are very good at hiding and can often hide in very small spaces.
  • Cats are very good at hunting and can catch mice, rats, and other small animals.
  • Cats are also very good at playing and can play with toys, other cats, and even dogs.
